Pollution




Causes

Pollution can be caused by various factors:
-Car and truck exhaust
-Factories
-Dust
-Pollen
-Mold spores
-Volcanoes
-Wildfires
-Burning fossil fuels, such as coal and petroleum
-Burning wood
-Chemical reactions in the air

Preventions

To prevent pollution, we can implement several measures:
-Driving your car less
-Keeping your car in good repair
-Turning off your engine
-Not burning your garbage
-Limiting backyards fire in the city
-Planting and caring for trees
-Reducing or eliminating fireplace and wood stove use
-Avoiding using gas-powered lawn and garden equipment

Facts

Here are some facts about pollution:
-Pollution is a global killer: Pollution affects over 100 million people, which is comparable to global diseases like malaria and HIV.
-Pollution can shorten life expectancy: From 1998-2021, particulate pollution cut global life expectancy short by 6.1 months.
-Pollution can increase the risk of cancer: People who live in places with high levels of air pollutants have a 20% higher risk of death from lung cancer.

Kleptocracy




Causes

Kleptocracy is more likely to occur when:
-Producer groups or their political representatives are shortsighted
-There is more foreign aid to the ruler
-The economy is reliant on the trade of natural resources

Preventions

Kleptocracy can be partially prevented with these actions:
-Create anti-corruption safeguards: Create and maintain anti-corruption safeguards.
-Build an activist network: Build and expand an anti-corruption and climate activist network.
-Bring the issue to the forefront of policy debates: Bring the issue of kleptocracy to the forefront of new policy debates.

Facts

Here are some facts about kleptocracy:
-Power resource: Kleptocracy is a system that uses coercion as its primary power resource.
-Power-over: Kleptocracy is a system where power-over delivers empowerment for the powerful at the expense of the compliant other.
-Structural bias: The kleptocratic status quo entails an extreme structural bias in favor of the powerful.
-Globalization: Over the last 30 years, as globalization has expanded, transnational kleptocracy networks have also become more expansive.

Bad Infrastructure




Causes

Poor infrastructure can be caused by many factors, including:
-Lack of funding
-Poor planning
-Corruption
-Lack of skilled personnel
-Poor repair and maintenance
-Grant dependency
-Lack of by-laws
-Inefficiency of developmental labor

Preventions

Here are some ways to improve infrastructure quality:
-Use clear, timely, and transparent decision-making, supported by strong evidence-based planning, clear prioritization, and best practice technical design and delivery.
-Plan for long-term governance and management, and consider the long-term impacts, risks, and trade-offs.
-Maintain an asset register, backed by a condition report on each asset, to reduce maintenance costs and increase the life span and quality of the infrastructure.

Facts

Here are some facts about bad infrastructure:
-include building collapses, water main breaks, gas pipe ruptures, dam failures, steam pipe explosions, and communications infrastructure failures.
-Aging, erosion, and damage due to weather-driven events and varmints: can damage natural gas, electricity, and other infrastructure over time.
-Lack of infrastructure project sponsorship and leadership: can cause failure in a project.
